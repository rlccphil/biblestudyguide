Portrait of a New Creation

A Five-Day Devotional Journey
✦

Welcome to Your Five-Day Devotional

This devotional is based on the sermon "Portrait of a New Creation." Over the next five days, you'll explore what it truly means to be transformed in Christ — not just in belief, but in the whole direction of your life. Each day is a short, intentional encounter with God's Word.

📖

Read the Scripture

Begin with God's Word. Let it settle in your heart before you read anything else on the page.

💡

Explore the Explanation

A short reflection helps you understand the passage in context and how it applies to life today.

✍️

Write & Send Your Notes

Use the journal space to write your honest response to the reflection prompt. When you're done, you can email your note to yourself or share it with a mentor, spiritual director, or Life Group leader.

👥

Discuss & Apply

The discussion questions are great for small groups. Each day ends with one practical step to take before tomorrow.

💬 A Few Practical Tips

  1. Set aside 15–20 quiet minutes each day — early morning or before bed works well.
  2. Begin with a simple prayer: "Lord, open my eyes and my heart to what You want to show me today."
  3. Be honest in your journaling. God already knows your heart — writing helps you hear it too.
  4. Email your notes to yourself for safekeeping, or share them with a mentor, spiritual director, or Life Group leader using the "Email This Note" button on each day.
  5. Don't rush through all five days at once. One day at a time, one step at a time.
  6. Bring your questions to your Life Group or a trusted friend. Formation happens in community.
  7. End each session in prayer, asking God for the grace to live out the practical application.

Day 1 of 5

From Empty Pursuits to Lasting Purpose

📜 Today's Scripture
"They tell how you turned to God from idols to serve the living and true God, and to wait for his Son from heaven, whom he raised from the dead — Jesus, who rescues us from the coming wrath."
1 Thessalonians 1:9–10

Becoming a "new creation" is not merely an internal feeling; it is a fundamental redirection of your entire life's trajectory. In the ancient world, turning from idols was a public and often dangerous move, yet it signaled a total change in orientation — from dead traditions to a living relationship.


Today, our "idols" might look like the pursuit of financial security, social status, or personal comfort, but the call remains the same. When you are in Christ, your affections undergo a supernatural swap: the things you once prioritized lose their grip, replaced by a deep desire to glorify God. This shift marks the beginning of a life lived with a heavenly perspective — looking forward to the return of Jesus rather than being anchored by earthly anxieties.

Take a moment to look at your daily priorities. In what areas is your "new orientation" becoming most visible, and where are you still tempted to return to old "idols" like power or pleasure?


Your Response
  1. How does the concept of "turning to God from idols" apply to modern life compared to the biblical era?
  2. In what ways have you noticed your "affections" changing — loving what God loves and hating what He hates — since following Christ?
  3. What practical steps can we take to keep our focus on the "living and true God" when the world tries to distract us?

Identify one "modern idol" — such as excessive social media use or a preoccupation with work — and intentionally replace that time today with a specific act of service or prayer for someone in need.

Closing Prayer Lord, show me where my affections have drifted. Replace my empty pursuits with a deep desire for You. I want to live today oriented toward You alone.

Day 2 of 5

When You Can't Change Yourself on Your Own

📜 Today's Scripture
"For we know, brothers and sisters loved by God, that he has chosen you, because our gospel came to you not simply with words but also with power, with the Holy Spirit and deep conviction."
1 Thessalonians 1:4–5

The transformation of a believer is not a project of self-will or mere behavioral modification; it is the masterwork of the Holy Spirit. This "new formation" begins the moment you trust in Jesus, as the Spirit moves into your life to reshape your character into the likeness of Christ.


This process is often felt as a "deep conviction" that changes how you view your choices and motivations. Instead of being driven by impulsive desires of the flesh, you are given a new inner compass that points toward faith, hope, and love. This spiritual architecture is most evident when you find yourself reacting with grace in situations where you previously would have responded with anger or selfishness.

Think about a recent challenge or conflict. How did the Holy Spirit prompt you to respond differently than you would have in the past?


Your Response
  1. Why is it impossible to become a "new creation" through human effort alone?
  2. How does the Holy Spirit's "deep conviction" feel different from simple human guilt?
  3. How can we become more sensitive to the Spirit's work of "new formation" in our daily routines?

Choose one person in your life who is difficult to love and ask the Holy Spirit to give you a specific, Christ-like desire for their well-being. Then act on it by sending an encouraging message today.

Closing Prayer Holy Spirit, I cannot change myself. I surrender the parts of me that still resist You. Work in me, reshape me, and make me more like Jesus today.

Day 3 of 5

Finding Joy When Life Falls Apart

📜 Today's Scripture
"You became imitators of us and of the Lord, for you welcomed the message in the midst of severe suffering with the joy given by the Holy Spirit."
1 Thessalonians 1:6

A common misconception is that becoming a "new creation" exempts us from the trials of this world, but the early church showed us a different reality. True spiritual formation is often forged in the furnace of "severe suffering," where the joy of the Holy Spirit acts as a supernatural anchor.


This joy is not a bubbly happiness dependent on circumstances, but a deep-seated peace that comes from knowing you are chosen and loved by God. When you handle hardship with this divine joy, you become an "imitator" of the Lord, showing the world that your hope is built on something far more substantial than temporary comfort. This resilience is one of the most unmistakable marks of the new creation living in a "now but not yet" world.

How has your faith provided you with a sense of joy or purpose even during seasons of personal struggle or suffering?


Your Response
  1. What is the difference between "happiness" and the "joy given by the Holy Spirit" mentioned in the text?
  2. Why does our response to suffering serve as such a powerful testimony to those around us?
  3. How can we support one another in "imitating the Lord" when things get difficult?

Visit or call someone you know who is currently going through a "severe suffering" and share how God has been your source of joy in your own trials.

Closing Prayer Lord, in the middle of what is hard right now, anchor me in Your joy. Let people see that my hope is not in my circumstances, but in You alone.

Day 4 of 5

Turning Your Daily Grind into a Divine Mission

📜 Today's Scripture
"The Lord's message rang out from you not only in Macedonia and Achaia — your faith in God has become known everywhere."
1 Thessalonians 1:8

The "new creation" does not just receive a new heart; it receives a new mission that turns every area of life into a sacred space. We often fall into the trap of separating our "spiritual" life from our "secular" life, but the Gospel breaks down those walls.


Whether you are at home, in the classroom, or at the office, you are standing on a platform designed for the expression of your faith. Your mission is not restricted to a church building; it is lived out in the way you conduct business, the way you study, and the way you treat your neighbors. As you walk in this purpose, your life becomes a "model" that rings out to those around you, making the message of Jesus visible and tangible.

If your workplace or school is your "mission field," what does the "message" of your life currently sound like to those who see you every day?


Your Response
  1. What does it mean to see "everything as a place of grace" rather than dividing life into sacred and secular?
  2. How can we "proclaim the Gospel" through our actions and character without always using words?
  3. In what ways can our small groups better support each other in our individual "mission fields" — work, home, school?

Tomorrow, before entering your "mission field" — work, school, or home — stop and pray: "Lord, show me how to make this place a platform for Your grace today."

Closing Prayer Lord, help me see that every space I occupy is Your mission field. Give me eyes to see the people around me the way You see them, and the courage to let my life speak.

Day 5 of 5

The Power of a Life That Inspires Others

📜 Today's Scripture
"And so you became a model to all the believers in Macedonia and Achaia… for they themselves report what kind of reception you gave us."
1 Thessalonians 1:7, 9

One of the most beautiful aspects of the "new creation" is that it is contagious — your transformation encourages the transformation of others. When people see a life that has been authentically changed — where labor is prompted by love and endurance is inspired by hope — they take notice.


You don't have to be perfect to be a "model"; you simply have to be unmistakable in your pursuit of Christ. This means viewing people not as "potential converts" to be won over, but as individuals whom Christ deeply loves and cares for. As you live out the truth of the Gospel in your relationships, you provide a "report" to the world of God's power to make all things new.

Who has been a "model" of the new creation for you, and what specific characteristics of their life moved you closer to God?


Your Response
  1. Why is it important that the new creation is seen "imperfectly but unmistakably" in us?
  2. How does viewing people through the lens of Christ's love change the way we approach sharing our faith?
  3. What is one area of your character where you want to become a better "model" for other believers?

Write a short note of gratitude to someone who has been a spiritual "model" to you, specifically mentioning a characteristic of Christ you see in them.

Closing Prayer Lord, thank You for the people You've placed in my life who show me what it looks like to follow You. Make me that kind of person for someone else. Let my life be a living portrait of Your grace.
