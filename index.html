It Is Finished

A 5-Day Devotional Journey

"It is finished." – John 19:30

How to Use This Devotional

This devotional is designed to help you slow down and truly encounter God through the finished work of Jesus on the cross. This is not just something to read—it is an invitation to reflect, pray, and allow God to shape your heart.

Simple Steps

  • 🙏 Start with prayer: Ask God to open your heart and make you receptive to His voice.
  • 📖 Read slowly: Let the truth of Scripture and the cross sink deeply into your life.
  • 🪞 Reflect honestly: Allow the Holy Spirit to reveal what is really happening in your heart.
  • 🙌 Pray sincerely: Respond to God personally, not just intellectually.
  • 📅 Practice daily: Transformation happens through consistent time with God.

Fasting & Prayer Guide

“When you fast… your Father… will reward you.” (Matthew 6:17–18)

Fasting is a way of intentionally setting aside something—often food—to create space to seek God more deeply. It is not about earning God’s favor but about depending on Him and aligning your heart with Him.

  • Start simple: You may skip one meal, fast half-day, or give up distractions like social media.
  • Replace, don’t remove: Use that time to pray, read Scripture, and be still before God.
  • Focus your heart: Pray about repentance, surrender, and gratitude for the cross.
  • Expect struggle: Hunger reminds you to turn your attention back to God.
  • Stay grace-filled: This is not about perfection but about drawing near to God.
Lord, as I begin this journey, draw my heart closer to You. Teach me to depend on You and to treasure what Jesus has done for me on the cross. Amen.

Mirror & Magnifier

Scripture: 1 Corinthians 1:18

“The message of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is the power of God.”

When you look at the cross, it becomes both a mirror and a magnifier. It reflects your true condition—your sin, brokenness, and need for God. At the same time, it magnifies the grace and power of God in a way nothing else can. This can feel uncomfortable because it exposes what you would rather hide. But this is where transformation begins.

The cross shows that your sin is more serious than you think—but God’s grace is greater than you imagine. You are not left in condemnation. Instead, through Christ, you are invited into hope, forgiveness, and change.

Father, help me to see myself clearly through the cross, not with fear but with hope. Transform me by Your grace. Amen.

Perfectly Perfect

Scripture: John 13:35

“By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you love one another.”

When Jesus said, “It is finished,” He completed the work of salvation. Nothing needs to be added to what He has done. Yet, your life is still being shaped. You are being formed into the likeness of Christ—what the devotional describes as being made “perfectly perfect.”

This does not mean you are instantly flawless. It means God is actively working in you. Through love, service, and obedience, your life begins to reflect Jesus more and more. The Holy Spirit helps you live this out, even when it is difficult.

Lord, thank You that Your work is complete, yet You are still working in me. Help me to love others as You have loved me. Amen.

Eyes to See

Scripture: John 9:25

“I was blind but now I see!”

Spiritual sight is not about intelligence or knowledge—it is about humility. Many who physically saw Jesus did not recognize Him, while those who were blind perceived who He truly was.

You may be familiar with church, Scripture, or spiritual things, yet still miss Jesus if your heart is closed. Pride, pain, or distractions can blind you. But when you come to Him honestly, He opens your eyes and helps you see clearly.

Lord, open my eyes. Remove anything that keeps me from seeing You clearly and following You fully. Amen.

For God's Glory

Scripture: John 11:4

“It is for God’s glory…”

Hardship often causes you to question God. But Jesus reveals that even suffering has purpose. The struggles you face are not meaningless—they can become a platform for God’s work and glory.

What you experience as confusion or pain may be part of a bigger story that you cannot yet see. God is able to redeem even the hardest moments and use them to reveal His power and goodness.

God, help me trust You even when I don’t understand. Use my life for Your glory. Amen.

Posture Matters

Scripture: Luke 18:13

“God, have mercy on me, a sinner.”

Your posture before God matters deeply. The difference between pride and humility determines how you experience Him. Those who come with self-reliance remain distant, while those who come with humility receive grace.

Like the tax collector and the thief on the cross, when you come honestly before God, acknowledging your need, you find mercy and acceptance. God welcomes a humble heart.

Lord, give me a humble and dependent heart. Help me to rely on You daily. Amen.
